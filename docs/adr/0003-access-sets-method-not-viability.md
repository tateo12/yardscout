# Access sets the placement method, not viability

The operator confirmed Units are **backed in when there is drive access, or craned in when there isn't.** Therefore a Parcel with no side-yard Access is still viable — it becomes a crane job. Access no longer gates the Score; it sorts a viable Parcel into a Placement method and cost tier: green-drive (room + access, cheapest), green-crane (room, no access, costs more and needs an on-site overhead/power-line check), red (no room to set the Unit at all).

Consequence: the knock list is broader than a drive-only model would produce (tight-side-yard lots are crane candidates, not rejects), and the Score's value shifts from in/out to prioritizing cheap drive-in wins over more expensive crane jobs. Overhead power lines are the crane-killer the field must confirm, the way fences are for backing in — neither is in the data.
